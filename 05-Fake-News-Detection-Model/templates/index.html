About Us

(Our Battle Against Fake News)

The aim of the fake news project is to help news readers to identify bias and misinformation in news articles in a quick and reliable fashion. We have collected news articles with veracity labels from fact-checking websites and used them to train text classification systems to detect fake from real news. You can paste a piece of text and examine its similarity to our collection of true vs. false news articles, or to news from four different types/genre. Enjoy testing!.

How It Works

( How the Model Predict the Information )

  • Text Preprocessing
    The input news text is preprocessed to remove any stop words and perform other text cleaning operations.
  • TF-IDF Vectorization
    The preprocessed text is transformed into a numerical representation using TF-IDF vectorization. This process converts the text into a matrix where each row represents a document and each column represents a unique word in the corpus. The values in the matrix represent the importance of each word in the document relative to the entire corpus.
  • Model Prediction
    The TF-IDF transformed text is fed into the trained Passive Aggressive Classifier model. The model then assigns a label to the input text based on its learned decision boundaries. In this case, the labels are typically 'FAKE' or 'REAL' for fake and true news, respectively.
  • Output
    The model's prediction (either 'FAKE' or 'REAL') is returned as the output, indicating whether the input news text is classified as fake or true.
